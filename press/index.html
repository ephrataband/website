In 2011 Seattle musician and filmmaker Brady Hall had fifteen songs written and recorded with no vocals. After producing, recording and performing the instrumentation for the first La Sera album with Katy Goodman of Vivian Girls in 2010 he spent the next year creating this new music in his spare time. The hunt for a vocalist to finish this new material ended when Skadi von Reis responded to Hall’s Craigslist ad after a summer spent wrangling horses in the hills of Montana. Von Reis and Hall recorded the vocals for the songs in the early months of 2012 and after a summer break recruited friends Ben Bromage and Jules jones to form a real band. A few months of hard work and continual tinkering of the harmonies later they had successfully translated the ambitious material to a live setting and began playing out.

In two short years Ephrata has shared the stage with bands like Dead Meadow, Yuck, Built To Spill, Chappo, Russian Red, Dutchess and the Duke, Frankie Rose at the Capitol Hill Block Party, Neumos, The Crocodile and SXSW. They record their own music, make their own videos and take their own photos.

Ephrata’s music has been compared to Slowdive, My Bloody Valentine, Warpaint and Cocteau Twins.
